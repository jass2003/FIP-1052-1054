Experience Wilderness

Summary of Experience Wilderness

CANADA'S OFFICIAL BIRD; THE CANADA JAY AND OF COURSE THE CHICK-A-DEE! TOUCHING WILDLIFE.

Famous birds that will eat seeds from your hand or your hat are the Canada Jay (aka the Gray Jay or the Whisky Jack) and Boreal Chickadee.

For the Aficionado Birder check the latest winter birding report for what is being seen and where.

  1. Algonquin Logging Museum Trail
  2. Spruce Bog Boardwalk Trail
  3. Mew Lake Old Railway Trail

We Provide Free to Use for our
- Pro IceHiking Crampons and Walking Poles
- Park Pass from our Office
- Bird seeds
- Please never scatter or leave seed behind on the trails.

Skiing & Snowshoeing

Summary of Skiing & Snowshoeing

Tranquil with peacefulness. Algonquin Park has International quality ski trails; track set and skating lane.

48 kilometres of groomed and 23 kilometres of non-groomed cross-country ski trails available in three low-usage trail networks, typically December to early April. Check the latest ski trail conditions If you cannot go skiing you can always go IceHiking.

Serene spots for Snowshoeing:

  1. Algonquin Logging Museum Trail
  2. Spruce Bog Boardwalk Trail
  3. Mew Lake Old Railway Trail

Provided Free to use:
- Wax-less cross-country skis, boots and poles.
- Park Pass from our Office
- Pro snowshoes
- Park Pass from our Office

Summary of ALGONQUIN PARK

ALGONQUIN VISITOR CENTER

Pristine and natural snow covered Algonquin Park is home to world-class venues and We provide the Free Park Pass.
